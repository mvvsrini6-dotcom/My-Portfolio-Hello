01 — The Problem

SCATTERED workflows

Creative studios were spending more time managing tasks, updates, client communication, and follow-ups than actually focusing on the work itself. Existing systems often relied on scattered spreadsheets, emails, chat threads, and multiple disconnected tools, creating friction across the workflow.

"I spend more time managing the work than actually creating it."

Fuse was designed to bridge this gap — bringing projects, people, payments, and communication into one clear and intuitive workspace. The challenge was creating a system that feels effortless for growing studios while remaining powerful enough for complex workflows.

The core question driving the design: How do you make meaningful connection feel as easy as a scroll?

04 — The Solution

DESIGN for clarity

Fuse reimagines studio management as a unified, intuitive system where projects, teams, payments, and communication exist in one clear workflow.

The design focuses on reducing friction, improving visibility, and helping creative teams spend less time managing work and more time creating.

01
Project Dashboard

A centralized view of all ongoing projects, deadlines, and progress to keep teams aligned.

02
Task Management

Clear task flows with priorities, assignments, and status tracking to reduce confusion.

03

Integrated communication and updates to keep everyone on the same page.

04

Manage invoices, payments, and financial status directly within the workflow.

06

A clean, consistent UI system designed for clarity, scalability, and ease of use.

05 — Outcome

LEARNINGS from the process

01
Clarity over Features
Simplifying workflows had a bigger impact than adding more functionality.
02
Unified Systems Matter
Bringing tasks, communication, and payments together reduces friction significantly.
03
Design for Real Workflows
Understanding how studios actually operate helped shape better solutions.
04
Iteration is Key
Multiple refinements were needed to improve usability and structure.
Reflection

This project helped me understand the importance of designing for real-world workflows rather than ideal scenarios. The biggest learning was that clarity and simplicity have a greater impact than adding more features.

If I were to improve this further, I would validate the design through usability testing with actual studio teams and refine the system based on real usage feedback.
